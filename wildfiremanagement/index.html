3. On the Line: Suppression Tactics & Resources

When a fire must be fought, commanders deploy a range of strategies and resources. The choice depends on fire intensity, terrain, and values at risk.

Core Attack Strategies

Direct Attack

Working directly on the fire's edge. Used for low-intensity fires.

Parallel Attack

Building a line near the fire's edge and burning out the fuel in between.

Indirect Attack

Building a line far from the fire, using terrain or roads as an anchor.

4. Living with Fire: The WUI & Defensible Space

As more communities are built in the Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI), adapting to fire is essential. This starts with managing fuel in the landscape and right up to the home itself.

The Power of Defensible Space

A home's survival is often determined by its immediate surroundings. The "Immediate Zone" is most critical, as it's the primary defense against embers, which cause the majority of home ignitions.
