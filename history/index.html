Debunking the "Pristine Wilderness" Myth

The story of American forests is often simplified: a vast, untouched wilderness met by settlers. This interactive chapter reveals a more complex truth. The forests encountered by colonists were not pristine, but landscapes actively shaped for millennia by Indigenous peoples through fire and agriculture. This application traces the four major eras of human-forest interaction, revealing how different cultures and technologies have continuously reshaped the continent's ecosystems.
